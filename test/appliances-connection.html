Washers
Finding the perfect clothes washer for your home can be a challenge, due to all the different options to consider when determining what exactly meets your needs. Each family is different, so think about the size and feature you or your family will require before purchasing. Appliances Connection can help.

Appliances Connection carries top brands, like Amana, LG, and Daewoo, to name a few. If you have any questions or need help choosing the right washer for your laundry room, contact us at 800-299-9470.

What is the right washer for you?
There are two types of washing machines: top loading and front loading. Top loading is not as energy-efficient as a front loader; however, a front loader generally has a longer wash cycle.
Top loading washers have a faster wash cycle and lighter in weight; however, they use more water and laundry detergent and are harder on your clothes. Front loading washers have a cleaner washer and use less detergent; however, they are much heavier and are typically more expensive on purchase.

Main Points of Consideration:
When determining which type is right for you, you first should consider what size and budget meets your need. If you live in a studio apartment, a large appliance may not be the right fit. Additionally, if you budgeted for a basic top-loading washer, you probably shouldn’t be considering next-gen models with advanced sensors that are twice the price.
Capacity is also important, which depends on the size of your home and family. For small family or apartment dwellers, a compact or stackable washer is sufficient. For larger families, look at the volume of laundry you go through to consider the capacity that fits your needs best. Volume is the amount of noise a machine generates during a spin cycle, so if your machine is next to a bedroom or on the second floor, a washer with quiet settings is your best choice.
Length of wash cycle is important to consider, especially if you are always on the run. Top loaders are generally your best choice if you are seeking a shorter wash cycle. Energy-efficiency is also important to consider, which will save energy as well as extend the life of your clothes.

Features to Look For:
Delay-starts allow you to set your washing machine before you head to bed. Automatic water level selects the exact amount of water for each load to prevent overfilling.
Internal water heaters reduce the amount necessary from your home heater.
